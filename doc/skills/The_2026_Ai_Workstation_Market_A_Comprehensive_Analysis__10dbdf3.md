### Key Insights
- **Puget Systems:** Known for its meticulous build quality and extensive performance testing, Puget Systems offers a range of AI development workstations. Their systems are optimized for popular AI frameworks and can be configured with single or multiple NVIDIA GPUs, supporting both AMD Ryzen/Threadripper and Intel Core/Xeon CPUs [8].
    - **BOXX Technologies:** BOXX focuses on building extreme-performance workstations for various professional fields, including AI. They offer systems based on AMD's Threadripper PRO platform with support for up to four high-end NVIDIA RTX PRO GPUs, housed in custom-designed chassis with advanced liquid cooling solutions [9].
    - **System76:** As a champion of open-source hardware and software, System76 provides a unique offering with its Thelio line of workstations. These systems run on their own Ubuntu-based Pop!_OS and feature custom-engineered thermal solutions. They offer a diverse range of configurations, from ARM-based workstations to powerful multi-GPU systems based on AMD Threadripper PRO [10].
    - **HP:** The HP Z series of workstations includes a variety of desktop and mobile solutions designed for AI workloads. At CES 2026, HP announced new models like the ZBook Ultra G1a and the Z2 Mini G1a, emphasizing mobile and compact AI performance [11].
    - **Dell:** Dell's Precision workstations are specifically marketed for AI development, offering scalable solutions from single-developer desktops to powerful towers capable of handling complex AI initiatives. Their systems are available with both Intel Core Ultra and Xeon processors, paired with NVIDIA RTX PRO GPUs [12].
    - **Lenovo:** Lenovo's ThinkStation and ThinkCentre lines are also being positioned for the AI era. The newly announced ThinkCentre X Tower, for example, supports dual GPUs and a mysterious "AI Fusion Card," highlighting the company's focus on innovative AI hardware solutions [13].
